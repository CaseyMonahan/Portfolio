Fresh Gear

Designing a clothing storage solution to keep your clothes smelling and feeling clean

Problem: Sam runs 5 days a week. Since he only does laundry every 2 weeks, he often wears running shorts multiple times. He doesn’t know where to put them after he wears them once, so they pile up on his floor and smell gross, especially after a few days.

Solution: Intermediate Clothing Cleaning - remove smell and dampness so clothing can be worn again FreshGear allows already worn but not dirty clothing to dry out and return to freshness for re-wear.

FreshGear: Integrated Product Design, Fall 2013

Teammates: Jordyn Burger (Olin), Mike Avagianos (MassArt), Bob Son, Seung Hwan Shin, and Trevor Sullivan (Babson)

IPD was a collaborative class between Olin, Babson, and Massachusetts College of Art and Design. The aim was to develop a product for “the sustainable home” and produce a looks-like model, a works-like prototype, and a complementary business model.

Thanks to instructors Ben Linder (Olin) and Sebastian Fixson (Babson), and teaching assistant Kathryn Brookshier (Olin)
